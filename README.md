## 📋 Executive Summary

This project presents a comprehensive strategic audit for **Eniac**, a Spanish Apple Premium Reseller, evaluating the potential acquisition of **Magist**, a prominent Brazilian e-commerce marketplace.

The final recommendation is a **"Conditional YES"**. While Magist offers significant scalability within the São Paulo tech hub, the audit revealed critical infrastructure gaps and security risks across Brazil's broader regions. A successful merger is only recommended if followed by a structured 3-phase roadmap to align Magist's marketplace model with Eniac's premium brand standards.

## 🔍 Key Strategic Insights

### 1. Portfolio Mismatch & Brand Alignment
Despite being a "tech" marketplace, high-end electronics account for only **21% of revenue**. The core assortment consists of low-cost items (<100€). For Eniac to succeed, a strategic shift toward premium segment positioning within the platform is mandatory.

### 2. The Punctuality Illusion
Magist reports a **93% on-time delivery rate** — but the data audit reveals this is based on artificially extended system estimates of 25–30 days. The actual delivery time for tech products is **13 days** — far too slow for a premium customer experience.

### 3. "Two-Speed Brazil": The Geospatial Performance Gap
The analysis identifies a stark contrast in operational efficiency:

| Region | Sellers | Avg. Delivery |
|--------|---------|--------------|
| São Paulo | 59% of all sellers | **9 days** |
| North / Northeast | Underserved | **23–27 days** |

Long-haul routes to the North are compromised by extreme security risks — approximately **22,000 cargo thefts per year**.

---

## 🗺️ Strategic Roadmap

To mitigate risks and ensure ROI, the fusion must follow three phases:

1. **Phased Expansion** — Initial focus exclusively on the Sudeste/South regions to leverage existing efficiency
2. **Regional Fulfillment** — Construction of dedicated logistics hubs in the North to bypass high-risk transit routes
3. **Service Tiering** — Implementation of a Premium Tier (5–7 days) vs. Standard Tier (15–20 days) to manage customer expectations
